Website Evaluations

Yale School of Art Official Webpage

This website is extremely clutered, has confusing/distracting visuals, and no consitency of content or styling. Visitors are left wondering how this could possibly be the school of arts official webpage. This is a great example of a BAD webpage.

  • Contrast: Most words is readable, dispite the randomly bolded words. With one notable exception, the white text on the visualy distracting gif can be difficult to read.
  • Repetition: Almost no noticable repetition is used except for a few repeating section backgrounds, all other design choices seem randomly generated
  • Alignment: Nothing seems to line up in any meaningful way.
  • Proximity: The entire page feels as if each individual section takes up entirely too much space and is far too spread out

Knapsack Official Webpage

Contrast is used to immediatly draw your eyes towards their sales pitch and further information. As you scroll you are greeted with pleasant animations and simple information formatted to perfection. This is a great example of a GOOD webpage

  • Contrast: Contrast is used to highlight important information for a pleasurable reading experience.
  • Repetition:Formatting is repeated as you scroll down the page so readers will intuitively know where to look for the desired information
  • Alignment: Every element is aligned in a meaningful way. This provides visitors with a sense of cohesion
  • Proximity: Each section takes up about a screens worth of space leading to intuitive navigation of the page.
